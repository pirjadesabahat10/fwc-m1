\section*{Question 2}
\begin{enumerate}

\item[\textbf{2.1}]
A bag contains 5 red balls and some blue balls.
If the probability of drawing a blue ball from the bag is three times that of drawing a red ball,
find the number of blue balls in the bag.

\item[\textbf{2.2}]
The 5\textsuperscript{th} and 15\textsuperscript{th} terms of an A.P.\ are 13 and $-17$ respectively.
Find the sum of the first 21 terms of the A.P.

\item[\textbf{2.3}]
Using Euclid's Division Algorithm, find the HCF of 225 and 867.

\item[\textbf{2.4}]
If the point $(0,2)$ is equidistant from the points $(3,4)$ and $(4,k)$,
find the value of $k$.

\item[\textbf{2.5}]
Find the value of $a$ for which the pair of linear equations
$4x + ay = 14 \quad \text{and} \quad 2x + 3y = 7$
has infinitely many solutions.

\item[\textbf{2.6}]
A card is drawn at random from a well-shuffled pack of 52 playing cards.
Find the probability of getting:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item a red king
    \item a queen or a jack
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
